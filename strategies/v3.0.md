# Strategy v3.0

Date: 2026-03-11

## Intent

`v3.0` replaces the hand-scored regime filter with a Hidden Markov Model that classifies ETH/USD into four latent market regimes and only allows long exposure when the probabilistic regime state and deterministic execution checks agree.

This remains a research-phase strategy:

- historical data only for this cycle
- long-only execution because Alpaca crypto spot cannot be sold short
- deterministic risk and execution rules still override the analyst

## Core Model

The strategy fits a 4-state Gaussian HMM on rolling 15-minute ETH/USD features:

- 1-bar log return
- 3-bar log return
- realized volatility
- ATR(14) in basis points
- ATR percentile
- 15-minute range in basis points
- 8/21 EMA gap
- 8 EMA slope
- 21 EMA slope
- volume z-score

The hidden states are remapped after every retrain into:

- `bull_trend`
- `bear_trend`
- `quiet_range`
- `stress`

The model is retrained on a rolling 20-day window of 15-minute bars and refreshed every 96 completed 15-minute bars.

## Execution Logic

The HMM is the top-level gate. A long entry is allowed only when all of the following are true:

- active regime = `bull_trend`
- bull posterior probability >= `0.62`
- one-step bull continuation probability >= `0.58`
- higher-timeframe EMA structure is bullish
- ATR percentile stays inside the tradeable band `0.18` to `0.82`
- 1-minute momentum confirms
- 1-minute breakout and participation confirm
- cost-adjusted edge remains positive after estimated slippage and fees

The trade plan is wider than V2 and is volatility-driven:

- stop loss = `clamp(ATR14_15m * 1.25, 18, 60)` bps
- TP1 = `1.25R`
- max TP = `2.4R`
- trailing stop = `0.75R`
- time stop = `180` 1-minute bars

The strategy exits when:

- `stress` probability crosses `0.48`
- `bear_trend` probability crosses `0.52`
- bull continuation decays and short-term momentum breaks down
- stop, trailing stop, or time stop fires in the backtester

## Risk Defaults

- requested risk fraction: `0.0015`
- max requested notional fraction of cash: `0.08`
- max spread: `12` bps
- min expected edge: `1.2` bps after estimated costs

## Interpretation

`v3.0` achieved the intended HMM architecture but failed the first replay:

- it traded too often
- it was too willing to enter on `bull_trend` alone
- exits were too reactive and still left the strategy with negative expectancy
- the strategy materially underperformed the existing `v2.2` baseline

The next step is a bounded refinement, not an architectural reset. `v3.1` should keep the 4-state HMM design and tighten regime confirmation, risk, and exit behavior.
